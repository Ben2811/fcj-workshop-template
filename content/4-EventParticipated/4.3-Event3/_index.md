# Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day”

**Date & Time:** 09:00, 30 May, 2026

### Event Objectives

- Help members level up their AWS skills through a clear, gamified growth path
- Give FCAJ Challenger teams a stage to share the real, unfiltered story behind their hackathon projects
- Reinforce the soft skills (confidence, beating procrastination) that determine whether technical skills actually get used
- Showcase a community member's personal product build, applying engineering skills outside a typical enterprise context

### Speakers

- **Huynh Thai Linh** - *Level up your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci*
- **Team The Ballers** - FCAJ Challenger -*Hackathon "HA! a ton" "of fun" "and bug fixes" "and back pains" "and sleep deprivation"*
- **Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu** - *Why We Always Need Confidence*
- **Nghia Tran** - Product Egineer - *Tu Vi Dai Viet*
- *The Iceberg of Procrastination*

### Key Highlights

#### Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci

- Improving AWS skills doesn't have to mean grinding through dense documentation — **AWS Cloud Quest** turns core services into a gamified, hands-on RPG-style learning path, while tools like **Floci** help track and reinforce progress over time.
- The real growth loop is **learn → build → get feedback**, repeated on real services instead of passively watching tutorials.
- A practical path: pick a certification or skill goal, use Cloud Quest/Skill Builder and the Free Tier to get hands-on, then join a study group (like FCAJ) for accountability and faster troubleshooting.

#### Hackathon Retrospective — Team The Ballers (FCAJ Challenger)

- A refreshingly honest retrospective on what a hackathon actually feels like: real fun during ideation, but also relentless bug fixing, back pain from marathon sitting, and serious sleep deprivation near the deadline.
- Hackathons test more than coding speed — they test **scope control**, team communication under pressure, and the discipline to stop adding features and start polishing what already works.
- Takeaway: treat a hackathon like a tiny product cycle — plan the MVP first, protect a minimum of sleep, and expect the "fun" and the "pain" to arrive together.

#### Why We Always Need Confidence

- Many technically capable people hold themselves back not from lack of skill, but from lack of confidence — hesitating to share ideas, present work, or apply for opportunities.
- Confidence isn't a feeling you wait for before acting — it's built through small wins, preparation, and repeated exposure to slightly uncomfortable situations (speaking up in a meeting, presenting a demo, asking a "basic" question).
- Practical reframe: act first in small, low-risk ways, and let confidence catch up afterward rather than waiting for it to arrive first.

#### Tu Vi Dai Viet — A Personal Product Build

- Nghia Tran shared the journey of building **Tử Vi Đại Việt**, a product applying software engineering to a distinctly Vietnamese cultural domain — traditional tử vi (astrology) readings — rather than a typical SaaS or enterprise use case.
- The talk walked through the full product-engineer path: validating the idea, shipping a working version, and iterating based on real user feedback.
- Takeaway: meaningful side projects don't have to chase trends — a domain you personally care about (culture, hobbies, community) is often the best source of product ideas and motivation to actually finish something.

#### The Iceberg of Procrastination

- Procrastination is usually treated as a discipline failure, but the visible "avoiding the task" behavior is only the tip of the iceberg.
- Underneath the surface sit the real causes: fear of failure, perfectionism, unclear next steps, or a task that simply feels unpleasant to start.
- Takeaway: fixing procrastination means addressing what's under the waterline — clarifying the next concrete step or the underlying fear — rather than just trying to "push through" harder.

### Key Takeaways

#### Skill Growth Needs a Loop, Not Just Content Consumption

- Real AWS skill-building comes from build-and-get-feedback cycles (like Cloud Quest), not passive tutorial watching
- Community and study groups shorten the feedback loop significantly

#### Technical Work Is Only Half the Hackathon

- Scope discipline, team communication, and basic physical/mental endurance decide outcomes as much as coding ability
- Planning for the human cost (sleep, stress) up front avoids the worst of the "back pain and sleep deprivation" experience

#### Soft Skills Are the Multiplier on Technical Skills

- Confidence and beating procrastination aren't "nice to haves" — they determine whether skills built elsewhere actually get used and shown
- Both are solved the same way: small, low-risk action first, insight/motivation second — not the other way around

#### Personal Projects Are a Legitimate Growth Path

- A culturally or personally meaningful project (like Tu Vi Dai Viet) builds real end-to-end product experience without needing a corporate use case

### Applying to Work

- **Set one concrete AWS skill/certification goal** and pair it with hands-on practice via Cloud Quest, Skill Builder, or the Free Tier
- **Scope any timeboxed sprint like a hackathon**: define the MVP first, and deliberately protect time to rest, not just to build
- **Practice visible confidence in small doses**: volunteer for one demo, share one idea in a meeting, ask one "obvious" question out loud
- **Start (or revisit) a personal project** in a domain you actually care about, to practice full end-to-end product thinking
- **Name the real blocker behind procrastination** on a stuck task instead of just trying to "focus harder"

### Event Experience

Attending this session of **“FCAJ Community Day”** felt very different from a pure technical deep-dive — it was as much about mindset and endurance as it was about AWS or software skills. Key experiences included:

#### Seeing a clear, gamified path to leveling up, instead of a vague "learn more AWS" goal
- Huynh Thai Linh's talk turned "get better at AWS" into a concrete loop of learning, building, and getting feedback using Cloud Quest and Floci alongside a study group.

#### Getting the unfiltered version of a hackathon, not just the demo highlight reel
- Team The Ballers' retrospective was refreshingly honest about the bug fixes, back pain, and sleep deprivation behind the polished final pitch — a healthier, more realistic picture of what building under pressure actually costs.

#### Being reminded that confidence is a practice, not a prerequisite
- Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu's talk reframed confidence as something built through small actions, not something you wait to feel before contributing.

#### Watching a personal idea become a real, shipped product
- Nghia Tran's walkthrough of Tu Vi Dai Viet was a good reminder that engineering skill applied to a personally meaningful idea — even a culture-rooted one — is still a legitimate, valuable product-building exercise.

#### Understanding procrastination as a symptom, not the root problem
- The Iceberg of Procrastination talk gave a simple mental model for digging into *why* a task keeps getting pushed off, instead of just feeling guilty about it.

#### Lessons learned
- Technical growth and soft-skill growth (confidence, beating procrastination) move together — neither one alone is enough.
- Hackathon and project stories are more useful when they include the hard parts, not just the win.
- A personally meaningful side project can be just as valuable for growth as a "resume-friendly" one.
