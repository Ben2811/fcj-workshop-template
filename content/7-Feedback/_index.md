> Here, you can freely share your personal opinions about your experience participating in the First Cloud AI Journey program. This will help the FCAJ team improve any shortcomings based on the following aspects:

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The working environment is very friendly and open. FCAJ members are always willing to help whenever I encounter difficulties, even outside working hours. The workspace is tidy and comfortable, helping me focus better.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**  
The mentor provides very detailed guidance, explains clearly when I don’t understand, and always encourages me to ask questions. The admin team supports administrative tasks, provides necessary documents, and creates favorable conditions for me to work effectively. I especially appreciate that the mentor allows me to try and solve problems myself instead of just giving the answer.

**3. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
During the internship, I learned many new skills such as using project management tools, teamwork skills, and professional communication in a corporate environment. The mentor also shared valuable real-world experiences that helped me better plan my career path.

**4. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
The company culture is very positive: everyone respects each other, works seriously but still keeps things enjoyable. When there are urgent projects, everyone works together and supports one another regardless of their position. This made me feel like a real part of the team, even as an intern.

**5. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
The company provides an internship allowance and offers flexible working hours when needed. In addition, having the opportunity to join internal training sessions is a big plus. You could join FCAJ Community Day to hear sharing about new knowledge & tips

---

### Additional Questions
**What did you find most satisfying during your internship?**  
Getting to take a real, already-shipped project (PeriodIQ) and own an entire slice of it end-to-end — from designing the DynamoDB tables, to building the Lambda Admin API, to shipping the Admin Panel frontend — instead of working on a disposable toy exercise. Writing the AWS Workshop chapter against real, live infrastructure (not a simplified rebuild) made the last few weeks feel like genuine engineering work rather than a classroom exercise.

**What do you think the company should improve for future interns?**  
I think probably nothing to change much at the moment, I can see the Admin & mentor trying their best to give us feedback at soon as possible.

**If recommending to a friend, would you suggest they intern here? Why or why not?**  
Yes — the combination of a solid AWS Study Group curriculum with a real hands-on team project at the end gives a much more complete picture of cloud engineering work than a curriculum-only internship would.

---

### Suggestions & Expectations
**Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?**  
Pairing each intern with their teammates earlier (even during the curriculum weeks) would make the transition into the team project in week 10 smoother, since role boundaries and integration points could be discussed in advance.

**Would you like to continue this program in the future?**  
Yes, I'd be interested in staying involved with FCAJ, whether as a participant in a follow-up track or as a mentor for future cohorts once I have more experience.

**Any other comments (free sharing):**  
Thank you to the FCAJ team and my mentor for the guidance throughout this program — it gave me a much clearer picture of what working in cloud engineering actually looks like.
